Lego Sentry Powered by a Raspberry Pi

Ever since I was a kid, I was fascinated by a mechanical sentry. Machines that would look at you, following your every move.

I needed a chassis that would able to handle the weight of the motors and move around with ease. I managed to use LEGO Technic pieces to achieve this, and balanced the weight of the motors on the front and back of the chassis. I limited the movement of the sentry to a 180 degree range, which was enough to track a person's face.

I needed to make a kickstand for the Pi, because the cable connected to the camera was too short to hide. I balanced the Pi on two beams, that pressed into the Pi and held it in place.

Equipment

  • Raspberry Pi 4 Model B
  • LEGO build HAT
  • LEGO Medium Linear Motor

Software

Python was the language I used to program the sentry. I used the OpenCV library to track faces. This was the easiest part of the project.
